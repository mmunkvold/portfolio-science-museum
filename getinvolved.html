get involved

Get involved

You can get involved in the museum and it's work in several different ways, not just by being a regular visitor.

You can support, donate, become a member, a volunteer, or you can even be an intern and work with our researchers and scientists.

Give support

There are various ways you can support the museum. Donations are very welcome and are an important way we keep this museum open and accessible to the whole community. You can also support us by donating items of interest to the museum’s collections. If you have some item or collection that you think others would enjoy, please let us know by contacting our Collections Department and they will be able to assist you.

There are various ways you can support the museum.

Become a member

You can become a member of the museum. By paying a small fee per year, you get membership status. This means you get some benefits like being invited to pre-shows of new exhibitions, get invited to special events and evenings at the museum.

You decide what kind of member you want to be, how much you want to pay and how much involved you want to be.

As a member or supporter, you will get to see behind the scenes of the museum and be invited to pre-shows of new exhibitions.

Become a volunteer

A number of people volunteer their time and effort to keep the displays in good order and ready for visitors to come and enjoy. Volunteering has its perks including getting to see behind the scenes of a working museum, access to staff-only lectures, and a monthly lunch where all staff and volunteers come together to discuss ideas for future exhibits and strategies for the museum.

As a volunteer you get to help out at the museum.

Apply for an internship

Are you interested in working in a museum? Do you enjoy the fun and excitement of sharing the wonders of nature with people? Well you could be just the right person to enjoy an internship at the museum.

You’ll be learning from a number of different academics and people who are passionate about science and sharing it with the wider community.

During an Internship you get to work with our researchers and scientists in the laboratory.
