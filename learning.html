Learning Activities for schools

As a community-driven museum, we want to work with schools to create places of learning and exploring. Our team are on hand to give your pupils guided tours of the museum, teach them in our learning laboratory, and provide great video presentations that will excite and inspire them.

The museum as a learning arena

The museum is a wonderful place to learn and explore!

Our many exhibitions can inspire many projects, and a visit to our research lab is really going to trigger a few inspiring scientists!

At the museum there are a lot to see and explore, like human hearts!

Learning Lab and Planetarium

In a house close to the main building, we have a learning laboratory where you and your pupils can experiment and explore. Here we will provide you with everything you need to have fun AND learn!

If you want to show your pupils what the sky is like, talk about planets and stars, we have a planetarium which is perfect!

We have different experiments for you and your pupils when you come!

Teaching Materials

Both before and after your visit to the museum we can help you with finding good teaching materials to help you plan and execute your visit here.

We even have a well-filled library you and your pupils can use during your visit.

Outside we have designated areas for experiments and fun.

Having fun in your classroom

If you don't have the opportunity to come and visit us, we can help you with planning a great science project or two in your classroom!

We can help you make your science projects even more exciting!

Let us help you plan your visit!
